Classics



Anna Karenina

by Leo Tolstoy

Published in 1877
Published by The Russian Messenger

The story focuses on Anna Karenina and her disreputable affair with Count Alexei Vronsky, leading to not only great scandal in high-society Saint Petersburg, but also the later disarray in their lives and her ultimate demise.




Jane Eyre

by Charlotte Bronte

Published on October 19, 1847
Published by Smith, Elder and Co.

Centering on the life of Jane Eyre, a plain, ugly, and unspecial girl, we follow her through all of life's up's and down's, with her trying to find acceptance, happiness, and love, anywhere and in anyone.


Fiction



The Secret History

by Donna Tartt

Published in September 1992
Published by Alfred A. Knopf

The story centers around the lives and friendships between six aberrant and intelligent students guided by their eccentric professor, redefining their lives through the works of ancient Greek philosophers. However, they eventually find themselves on a path of perilous secrecy and evil.




1984

by George Orwell

Published on June 8, 1949
Published by Secker & Warburg

A dystopian novella predicting the lives of the people in the future, 1984 centers around Winston Smith and his vexation and questioning of not only his government but his tyrant ruler, Big Brother.


Non-Fiction



The Diary of a Young Girl

by Anne Frank

Published on June 25, 1947
Published by Otto Frank

The Diary of a Young Girl is the diary of Anne Frank, a young Jewish girl living in Nazi Germany who was forced to go into hiding. She writes about her family and another family's lives living in a secret annex behind a fake bookshelf for 2 years until they are betrayed and taken away by Nazi soldiers to the Auschwitz concentration camp.




A Brief History of Time

by Stephen Hawking

Published in 1988
Published by Bantam Dell Publishing Group

In this book, Stephen Hawking explains many concepts of physics and astronomy that are very difficult to grasp for the average person like time, space, gravity, planets, stars, and blackholes, so that everyone can understand better the history and origins of our planet.
